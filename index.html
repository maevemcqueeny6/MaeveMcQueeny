About Me

When I was 10 years old sitting I remember sitting in front of the tv scrolling through the channels when I discovered her--My Giaconda. It was Angelina Jolie, in Hackers. Those lips made me linger on the channel, but the storyline made me stay.

Powerful goth kids picking the locks to invisible keys that hold all information. As a nosy younger sibling, I found myself salivating – and not just at Angelina Jolie this time.

I aspired to this level of resourcefulness, adroitness, and sedentariness.

But, alas, I was a 10 year old kid in the suburbs. And my parents didn't allow much internet play time.

I learned other things, other codes – the histories of different cultures and peoples. If I could not be Angelina Jolie in Hackers, I would resign myself to becoming Nicholas Cage in National Treasure. I learned a different hacker language, Russian, and double majored in History and Russian Literature and Language. I thought that would be enough. I would never pull back the curtain on computer programming (which would probably just reveal a zillion whizzing servers).

Out of college, I put my study of language to use at a public relations firm. I was able to apply my creativity by writing and creating video segments. However, after a while, my tasks lacked a certain element of problem-solving, and my natural curiosity began to wander.

I tried to supplement this curiosity in my private life, teaching myself Adobe Creative Suite, the principles of design, and front end web development. But I needed more.

That’s when I decided to enroll in a full stack web development program through the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.

Here, I learned full stack: HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, jQuery, Bootstrap, Express.js, React.js, Node.js, Database Theory, MongoDB, MySQL, Git, C#, ASP.Net, and more.

But those are just the technical skills. This course was also a bootcamp in soft skills. I poured over books to supplement what Angelina Jolie taught me all those years ago. I learned that half a byte is a nibble, I got lost at trying to learn circuits, and I about the copyleft in open source licensing.

From my classmates I learned the culture – with humble respect and humility, keeping in mind that the expert at anything was once a beginner. The look of shock on my classmates faces when I told them I had never seen any of the Star Wars franchise. I will live with that shame all my life.

Already I've achieved my goals and more, for every day is more challenging and rewarding than the last.
